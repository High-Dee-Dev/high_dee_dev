Our Origin

A personal pursuit turned purpose

Vela began as a personal project by designer Elara Vance. After a decade working for larger fashion houses, she grew frustrated with the industry's pace and waste. The constant churn of trends felt disconnected from how people actually lived and dressed.

In 2018, she started creating a small capsule wardrobe for herself—pieces that could be mixed, matched, and worn for years. Friends began asking for duplicates, and Vela slowly evolved from a personal experiment into a brand.

Today, we operate from a light-filled studio in Portland, Oregon. Every garment is still designed by Elara and a small team of pattern makers who share her commitment to quality and longevity.

Our Approach

Three principles guide everything we create.

Material Integrity

We source natural, durable fabrics from suppliers who prioritize environmental responsibility. Every textile is tested for hand-feel, wear, and aging.

Considered Design

Each silhouette undergoes months of wear-testing and refinement. We design for real bodies and real movement, not just runway proportions.

Intentional Production

We produce in small batches to minimize waste. Our manufacturing partners are paid fair wages and work in safe, regulated facilities.

Materials We Trust

European Linen

Sourced from Belgium and France. Known for its breathability, texture, and durability. Becomes softer with each wear and wash.

Organic Cotton

Grown without synthetic pesticides. We use medium-weight knits and woven canvases that hold their shape wash after wash.

Wool Blends

Responsibly sourced merino and lambswool blended with natural fibers for warmth without bulk. Ideal for transitional layers.

Silk Blends

A touch of silk adds subtle sheen and drape to our knitwear. We blend it with cotton for easier care and daily wear.
